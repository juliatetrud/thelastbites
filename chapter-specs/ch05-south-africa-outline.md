# Chapter 5 Outline — A Stew for the Crew

The midpoint chapter. Pip meets Iris, earns memory-gifting, and uses it for the first time as a chosen weapon. South Africa is the port; the Mamlambo is the monster; a chaotic uncle-chef and his potjie are the warmth. The chapter that pivots Pip's mission from *tasting* to *understanding why food matters to the living*.

This document is the **narrative outline** for the chapter. Implementation-grade detail (full dialogue, cinematic specs, memory-gifting UI) gets written separately when the chapter is being built.

**Working title:** *A Stew for the Crew.* Recipe-themed convention from Ch3 onward.

---

## Position in the arc

Chapter 5 is the **midpoint** of the seven-beat arc. Per the bible (`01-story-bible.md`):

> *The Mnemosyne stalls above the wreck of The Lethe. Iris squeezes through the floorboards. Pip helps her finish her unfinished meal by manifesting her family — earning memory-gifting. The lesson — moving on is reconnecting — pivots Pip's mission. From here, the puzzles get more emotional. He's not just tasting; he's understanding.*

- **Memory-gifting earned** — Pip's most important ability per the bible, learned in two beats: *receiving* a memory from Iris's switchblade (object-memory extending naturally), then *giving* it back to her in full clarity.
- **First deliberate use of memory-gifting against a monster.** The Mamlambo's brain-suck pulls Pip's memories involuntarily; Pip *reaches back* and weaponizes the warm Iris-family memory deliberately. Memory-gifting is introduced, then immediately escalated.
- **The midpoint pivot — moving on is reconnecting.** Iris's resolution rehearses Pip's own. The player feels the shape of the climax for the first time.
- **Cross-chapter inventory pattern continues.** The candle (Ch1) and matches (Ch3) were used in Ch4. Now Iris gives Pip her father's switchblade, which becomes load-bearing for Ch6's monster fight.
- **The chapter rhythm holds, with a structural twist.** Wordless traversal → monster encounter → chef encounter is preserved, but the *Iris beat sits before the monster encounter*, on the ship. Three settings instead of two: ship-traversal → ship-Iris → ship-Mamlambo → port-chef.

## Emotional arc

**What Pip carries in:** Object-memory and taste-memory abilities. The journal with three recipes (gravlaks/lefse, kodused kotletid, Sandy's red curry, Muhittin's muhammara — *four*). The lessons from Ch3 (*the dead can come back to feed the people they love*) and Ch4 (*the dead are alive in the food their grandchildren still cook*). His candle and matches still in his pocket. Pätu is with him.

**What Pip learns:** Some children are stuck because finishing means forgetting — and you can give them back what they're afraid to lose. Memory is *transferable*. He can receive it from an object that someone loved, and he can give it forward to the next person who needs it. *Moving on is not forgetting. Moving on is reconnecting.* He also learns that warmth, used deliberately, is a weapon some monsters cannot survive.

**What Pip carries out:** The most important ability he will gain. A switchblade Iris's father gave her, now in his pocket. A new recipe — the chef's parents' potjiekos. A taste-memory of a child watching his parents tend a fire under a black dutch oven. The understanding that *he is on his own version of Iris's story*, and that he, too, will eventually need to finish his meal.

The chapter's emotional center is the moment Pip gives Iris back her family. Not as a stranger's intervention — as a *gift returned*. He took the memory in by touching her father's switchblade; he gives it back in full clarity so she can want to go to them. *That's the lesson of the whole back half of the game, enacted once before Pip will need to do it for himself.*

## Setting

**The Mnemosyne, lower deck (wordless traversal).** A passage Pip hasn't been in before — old wood, low ceiling, the smell of damp. Storage on either side. The sea is visible through small portholes, the moon high. The ship is unusually still — *stalled*. Echo-creatures: **rats**, scuttling along the baseboards. They drain Pip's strength on touch (per the echo-creature rule in doc 02). Pip and Pätu must navigate around them.

**The lower-deck room.** A storage room at the end of the corridor. Old floorboards. Crates stacked along the walls. A single porthole window with a heavy iron clasp. This is where Iris emerges, where Pip and Iris talk, where the Mamlambo arrives, and where the puzzle is staged. **One room, three beats.**

**Saldanha Bay, South Africa.** A natural deep-water harbor on the Cape West Coast, north of Cape Town. Working fishing port, small-town texture, big sky. The ship docks at dusk. The cool-base palette holds; the regional shadow note is *deep red-ochre and sun-baked dust* — even though it's nightfall, the colors of the day are still in the walls and the dirt of the streets. Fynbos hills inland, the Atlantic to the west. **A single warm-amber pool: Johannes's fire**, visible from blocks away. Outdoor potjie-cooking is normal here; the chapter's central image (a black cast-iron pot on three legs over an open fire in a small courtyard) belongs to this place naturally.

**The chef's place.** Outdoor. A fire pit in a courtyard or small backyard, a black cast-iron pot suspended over it on three legs. A small kitchen behind, half-open to the air. A table set for friends — Pip and Pätu are not the only diners (the chapter's working title is *A Stew for the Crew*; the chef is feeding people). Spice jars on a shelf, herbs hanging from rafters, a battered wooden spoon stained with decades of stew.

**Henrik's kitchen, aboard the Mnemosyne.** Familiar from Ch1 and Ch4. Pip's destination at the chapter's end. He brings potjie back for Henrik and the crew.

## Beat 1 — Wordless traversal: the lower-deck rats

Pip and Pätu move through a part of the ship Pip hasn't been in before. The Mnemosyne is unusually still. The engines are quiet; the hull doesn't hum.

The corridor is narrow, low-ceilinged, lined with crates. **Echo-rats** scuttle along the baseboards — translucent warm-amber forms, darting from crate to crate, vanishing into the gaps. They are the residue of an old infestation, never properly addressed. *(Echo-creature: rats. Per doc 02, each variety has its own traversal flavor — rats *skitter and dart*, fast and unpredictable, low to the ground.)*

**Mechanic.** Pip moves through the corridor on the ground. Touching an echo-rat drains a tiny breath of strength (gentle, instant — per the echo-creature rule). The rats appear at unpredictable intervals. Pip can **float** above them when they cluster. Pätu pads alongside, low and watchful — she does not chase them. *(Pätu's discipline contrasts the Ch4 alley moment, where the echo-cats spooked her. Rats don't bother her. She's a cat who chooses what to fear.)*

The corridor ends at a heavy wooden door, slightly ajar. Pätu noses it open. The room beyond is dim — moonlight through one porthole, a stack of old crates, the smell of damp wood.

**No dialogue. Movement, atmosphere, the unusual stillness of the stalled ship. The traversal ends when Pip and Pätu step into the storage room.**

## Beat 2 — Iris emerges

The storage room is quiet. Pip drifts toward the porthole. Outside, the sea is glassy and unmoving. *(The player may register: the ship is over a wreck. We are above something.)*

A sound from the floorboards. *A squeeze, then a pop.*

A wet shape rises from between two boards in the floor — *a child, small, in Edwardian clothes, soaked from the sea.* Her hair is plastered to her head. Water is pooling on the wood around her. She blinks at Pip. Pip blinks back.

> IRIS: *Oh. Hello.*

Pip is too startled to speak for a beat. Then:

> PIP: *Are you... are you all right? You're soaked. I'll find you a—*

Pip turns to look for something — a cloth, a blanket, anything. Behind him, Iris **shakes off the water like a dog** — head, shoulders, whole body in a single fast shudder. Drops fly. By the time Pip turns back, she is dry. Her hair is settled. Her dress is unwrinkled.

Pip stares.

> IRIS: *Don't be rude. It's not polite to stare.*

Her speech is formal, slightly archaic. She holds herself the way a Victorian child in a portrait holds herself — straight-backed, hands loose at her sides. She looks Pip over with frank curiosity.

> IRIS: *You're a ghost.*
> PIP: *...yes. So are you?*
> IRIS: *Obviously.*

She is opaque. She looks like a living girl. Pip does not yet realize she is also a ghost — only that she's strange, and a little stuck-up, and was very recently underwater.

Pätu trots over and head-butts Iris's leg. Iris pets her — *expertly, the way someone who has loved a cat in a previous century pets a cat.*

> IRIS: *Hello. You're a good girl. Yes you are.*

Pätu purrs.

## Beat 3 — Iris explains (and Pip sees through her)

Iris stays with Pätu. Pip is across from her. They talk — the chapter's first long conversation, and the chapter's emotional throughline.

*(Open: the full dialogue, to be drafted when the chapter is built. The texture below is the shape.)*

She tells him her name is Iris. She died on a ship called *The Lethe* — an English ocean liner, a long time ago. The ship sank somewhere off the Horn of Africa. She was eating dinner with her family when it happened. She didn't finish her meal.

She has been at the wreck ever since. *The Mnemosyne* is stalled directly above it, and the proximity let her reach up. She squeezed through the floorboards. She doesn't usually get to go *above* the water. It's been a while.

Pip asks gently — *why didn't you finish your meal?*

Iris is quiet for a moment. She says: *because if I finish, I'll forget them.* She does not look at Pip when she says this. She is petting Pätu, who is now in her lap.

Pip notices something. Iris is *fading at the edges* — the wallpaper of the storage room is visible through her shoulder. **He understands now.** She is not opaque. She was; she's gone translucent because she's *sad*. (Mirrors Pip's own form blurring when frightened — bible canon. The player who has been paying attention catches it.)

> PIP: *...I forget things too sometimes. About the people I miss.*
> IRIS: *Then you understand why I won't.*

A pause. Then she remembers something. She reaches into a small pocket sewn into her pinafore and pulls out an object — *a small folding switchblade, mother-of-pearl handle, Edwardian make.*

> IRIS: *My father gave me this. For sharpening my pencils. He said a girl who studies should always be able to sharpen her own pencils.*

She holds it out to Pip. Not to give — to *show*.

> IRIS: *Would you like to hold it? I don't let just anyone.*

Pip takes the switchblade. **Object-memory triggers** — earned in Ch2, never used on someone else's loved object before this.

## Beat 4 — The memory through the switchblade

Pip closes his eyes.

A *dining room on a ship*. Edwardian. Long table, white tablecloths, candles. A father in a dark suit sits beside a girl in a white pinafore — *Iris*. He is showing her how to fold the switchblade closed. *"Always away from yourself, dear. Always away from your fingers."* He is smiling. The mother is across the table, laughing at something Iris's brother has just said. The brother — older, ~14 — is making a face. There are at least four other people at the table. A meal is half-eaten. A small dish of brussels sprouts sits beside Iris's plate, untouched. The mother glances at it and gestures, gently insistent — *eat your sprouts, dear.*

The dining room is warm. The light is amber. Everyone is alive.

The image holds.

The kitchen returns. Pip is sitting in the storage room with Iris, the switchblade in his open palm. Iris is watching him.

> IRIS: *You saw something.*
> PIP: *...your father. The dining room. Your mother told you to eat your sprouts.*

Iris stares at him. Her edges are no longer translucent.

> IRIS: *You saw them?*
> PIP: *I saw them.*

She is quiet for a long beat. Then, very quietly:

> IRIS: *...I'd forgotten what his voice sounded like.*

**The chapter's hinge.** Iris's grief is not that they are gone. Her grief is that *she has been forgetting them*. The thing she's been holding the meal against — *finishing means forgetting* — is already happening. She has been losing them slowly the whole time.

The journal entry forms in Pip's mind. *(The entry: Iris's family at dinner. Source: switchblade. Saved.)*

## Beat 5 — Memory-gifting, earned

Pip has the memory now. He looks at Iris. He understands what she needs.

**The memory-gifting input.** Pip's journal opens. The journal entries are arranged horizontally; **left and right arrows navigate** between memories. **Up arrow selects** the highlighted memory and sends it. *(This is consistent with the game's broader two-axis directional input — same simple grammar as movement, no new buttons.)* Pip selects the just-recorded *Iris's family at dinner* entry and presses up.

The memory leaves the journal. It flows through him — through his outstretched hand — *toward* Iris.

**Visual signature.** The same wavy transition used for Pip's other memories — the shimmer-and-bloom that triggers taste-memory and object-memory — but going *outward* from Pip rather than inward. The storage room dissolves through the wave. The dining room blooms in. Same animation language; reversed direction; the player learns the literacy by recognizing the familiar transition turned around.

The dining room manifests — *not as a vision Iris is seeing, but as a place that is, briefly, around her.* The long table with white tablecloths. The candles. Her father in his dark suit. Her mother. Her brother. The other guests. All of them mid-meal, alive, laughing. The light is warm-amber.

> *(Note: this is the chapter's biggest cinematic. The dining room is loud, full, joyful. Many faces. Long table. The kind of dinner a child remembers as the world.)*

Iris stands. She is no longer translucent. Her father turns toward her.

> FATHER: *There you are, darling. Come and finish your dinner.*

The mother smiles.

> MOTHER: *And eat your sprouts, too.*

Iris is crying — a child's clean crying, not a sad cry. She turns to Pip.

> IRIS: *Thank you, ghost-boy.*
> PIP: *Pip. My name is Pip.*
> IRIS: *Pip. Thank you.*

She closes her hand around his on the switchblade. Then she opens his fingers and presses the blade *into his palm.*

> IRIS: *Keep it. You'll need it. I think — I think where you're going, you'll need something to hold.*

*(Cross-chapter: the switchblade is now in Pip's pocket. It will be used in Ch6's monster fight. Pattern continues — Ch1's candle, Ch3's matches, Ch5's switchblade. Each chapter hands forward an object.)*

## Beat 6 — Iris finishes her meal

Iris turns toward the floorboards where she emerged. She kneels. She reaches between the boards.

A *squeeze, then a pop* — and she pulls something up from below. **A small plate of brussels sprouts, soaked from the sea.** They were on the floor of the wreck, untouched for over a century. They look perfect anyway — held in the moment they were left.

Iris holds the plate close, looking at the sprouts. She takes a breath.

She picks one up. Eats it.

Pip floats beside her. *(Visual: Pip is clearly off the ground in this image. The float is legible.)* *(Beat from your direction: he eats with her — they finish the meal together, because in the memory her mother told her to finish.)*

> IRIS: *They're cold.*
> PIP: *They're not bad. They're really not.*

*(Bible-locked line, slightly relocated — Pip says it about the shared meal, not as the act of eating-them-for-her. The line lands the same warmth.)*

They finish the sprouts together. A few each. The plate empties. The dining room around them is still there — her family is still gathered, still warm, still waiting.

Iris stands. She turns toward her family. Her father holds out his arm.

> FATHER: *Come on, darling.*

Iris walks toward the table. She does not turn back to Pip — she is allowed to leave without looking back. The dining room dims around her, fading slowly. The last thing visible is Iris taking her seat between her father and her brother, picking up her fork, beginning to eat.

The room is the storage room again. Pip is alone with Pätu. The plate is empty. The sea outside the porthole is still glassy.

The switchblade is in his pocket.

## Beat 7 — The Mamlambo

A loud thud. The ship rocks.

Pip stumbles. Pätu's fur stands up. From the porthole, a long shape is visible against the hull — *coiling, intelligent, vast.* Eyes the size of dinner plates. The Mamlambo.

Another thud. The ship rocks harder. Crates topple in the corridor outside the storage room. Items skid across the floor — a metal lantern, a coil of rope, a battered tin cup. Pip braces himself against a crate.

Through the porthole, the Mamlambo's body slithers past. Its head appears at the glass — eye to the porthole. Looking *in*.

Pätu hisses — *the canonical hiss, sharp and intentional.* This is a thing she fears.

Pip understands what this is. *Iris warned him.* The Mamlambo blocks the way off the ship. It is the chapter's monster.

**Pip cannot swim through water — only walls. He cannot leave the ship until the Mamlambo is gone.**

## Beat 8 — The puzzle: get the monster's attention, then send the memory

The chapter's cleverness puzzle, themed to the monster. *The Mamlambo is the brain-sucker. He takes what is inside the people he meets. You give him something he cannot keep down.*

**The puzzle has three movements:**

### (i) Get the monster's attention

The Mamlambo is rocking the ship and ramming the hull, but he hasn't fixed on Pip yet. The porthole is closed. Pip needs the creature *looking at him* for the memory-gifting to work.

**Mechanic:** Pip moves around the storage room, picking up small objects that have skidded in — the metal lantern, the tin cup, a brass hinge, a glass bottle. He must **open the porthole window** (heavy iron clasp, requires a beat of effort), then **throw an object** through it at the Mamlambo. The first object bounces off harmlessly; the creature notices. Its head whips toward the porthole. *Eye to the glass.*

*(Open: the exact input — single press to throw, choice of object, etc. To be designed when the chapter is built. No fail state — Pip can keep throwing objects until the Mamlambo turns.)*

### (ii) The brain-suck and the gift

The Mamlambo opens its mouth. Something invisible reaches *through the porthole* into Pip — a pull, a tug at the inside of his head. *He's trying to suck Pip's brain.*

But Pip is dead. There is nothing for the Mamlambo to take by force.

What the Mamlambo gets instead is *access*. The creature is now reading every memory in Pip's journal. **Pip feels it.**

This is the moment Pip *chooses*. He doesn't try to push the Mamlambo out. He **reaches back** — like he just did with Iris — and *sends* the warmest memory in his journal directly into the creature's pull. The Iris dining-room memory. The long table, the white tablecloths, the father's hands, the mother saying *eat your sprouts, too.*

**Mechanic:** the journal opens. Left/right arrows navigate, up arrow selects — same grammar as Beat 5. The player picks the memory to send. The Iris-dining-room entry is the right answer; other entries can be tried (none cause harm, none progress) — *(open: do other warm memories also work, or only the freshly-Iris one? Pull: only Iris works, because the Mamlambo took her family in the first place. The memory is what he stole made warm. He cannot keep it down. To be confirmed when the chapter is built.)*

### (iii) The recoil

The memory hits the Mamlambo. *He cannot hold it.* The creature shrieks — a sound that rattles the porthole glass — and recoils, body convulsing, head whipping away from the window. He is *disgusted* by the warmth. The brain-suck was searching for something hungry to take; he got *family* instead.

The Mamlambo retreats into the deep water. His body slides past the porthole one last time, then he is gone. The ship steadies.

In his wake, something floats up to the surface and bobs against the ship's hull — *a small canvas bag, cinched at the top, sodden but intact.* Pip can see it through the porthole.

> PIP (quietly): *From Iris.*

**The Lethe is right there. As the Mamlambo's churning settles, debris from the wreck surfaces.** The bag is brussels sprouts — Edwardian, century-pickled by the cold sea, somehow still intact. *Iris's last meal floating up to feed the next one.* Pip understands without needing it explained.

## Beat 9 — Off the ship, the chef, the cat strategy

Pip phases through the wall. Pätu trots out behind him through the door. They reach the dock. Pip retrieves the bag of brussels sprouts from the water (a small float-and-grab beat, since he can't swim).

The South African port at dusk. Cool-base palette, deep red-ochre shadows in the walls. Pätu sniffs the air. She trots ahead — purposeful, knowing. Pip follows.

She leads them through the streets of Saldanha Bay toward a single warm-amber light — a fire visible from blocks away, a chimney of smoke and the smell of slow-cooking meat. A small home with a courtyard. A black cast-iron pot suspended over an open fire on three legs. **A potjie.**

The chef is there, tending it. **Johannes Delport** — older, bearded, talking to himself, then singing a few lines, then talking again — a constant warm rumble. Spice jars on a shelf. Herbs hanging from the rafters of the half-open kitchen behind him.

Pätu trots into the courtyard.

> JOHANNES: *Ach, no, no, no — out, cat. Out. I am cooking. There is no scrap for you yet. Out.*

He waves a wooden spoon at her. Pätu retreats. She pads back to Pip, looks up at him, looks at the bag of brussels sprouts, looks back at the chef.

Pip understands. *The cat is the messenger.*

He takes the bag of brussels sprouts and ties it loosely around Pätu's neck — a tiny canvas pendant, just snug enough. Pätu walks back into the courtyard, head high, the bag swaying.

The chef looks up. Sees the cat with the bag.

> JOHANNES: *...what is this. What have you brought me, hmm? Brussels sprouts? Cat with brussels sprouts? Where did you get these? Ach — fine. Fine. You can stay. Come, cat. Come and watch.*

He laughs. He is a man who responds to absurdity by accepting it. He kneels, takes the bag from around Pätu's neck, and tips the sprouts onto a board beside his pot. Pätu settles down by the fire and starts purring.

Pip — invisible to the chef — slips into the courtyard behind her.

## Beat 10 — Cooking the potjie

The chapter's recipe-acquisition. Format C from `02-game-design.md` (ingredient logic), woven through a chaotic and warm scene. **The chef cannot see Pip — but he is so forgetful that Pip can help anyway.**

**The recipe — the chef's parents' potjiekos — built across the scene:**

The chef sings to Pätu. He talks to himself. He talks to the pot. He occasionally glances skyward as if speaking to someone else who isn't there. *(Open: the chef's voice texture, to be developed. He is talking constantly.)*

He starts assembling the stew — and immediately starts losing things. He puts a bunch of thyme down on the board, walks over to get the oxtail, and when he comes back the thyme is *(actually right where he left it, but he's looking elsewhere — Pip floats it gently into his line of sight)*. *"Ah, there you are."* He sets the potatoes aside, forgets which jar holds the paprika, mutters at the spice shelf. *Pip pulls the right jar to the front.* *"Aha! There you are."*

**The ingredients, surfaced one by one as the chef looks for them:**

1. **Oxtail** — already browning in the pot.
2. **Onions, garlic, carrots, potatoes** — chopped chaotically, dropped in a layer.
3. **Brussels sprouts** — *"And these — ach, what a gift. From the cat. You know what these are good for? Slow cooking. The bitterness goes. I haven't made it with sprouts in years. My mother used to —"* (He trails off. He doesn't finish the thought. He just keeps stirring.)
4. **Thyme, bay leaves, peppercorns, paprika, cumin, coriander seed** — a chaotic flurry of jars. Pip helps push the right ones forward as Johannes gestures vaguely.
5. **Beef stock, red wine, a splash of brandy** — *"For the soul, you see. The brandy is for the soul. Don't tell my doctor."*

Johannes closes the heavy lid on the potjie. He sits down beside the fire. Pätu is in his lap now. He scratches her ears.

> JOHANNES: *Now. We wait. The pot does the rest.*

The fire crackles. The smell of slow-cooking meat and brussels sprouts and paprika fills the courtyard.

## Beat 11 — The taste-memory: the child by the fire

After a long while of waiting (compressed cinematically), Johannes lifts the lid. Steam billows. He stirs, tastes a spoonful, nods. He ladles a small bowl. He sets it on a low table in the courtyard.

> JOHANNES: *For our cat-friend's friend, whoever they may be. You eat first, ghost.*

*(He says this as a joke, half to himself, half to the air. He doesn't know Pip is there. But the bowl is there. Pip takes it.)*

Pip eats. **The taste-memory triggers.**

The courtyard shimmers. Pip is in a different place — *a clearing in the South African veld, decades ago.* A fire is going. A black cast-iron pot hangs on three legs over the flames — the same pot, when it was new. A young couple are tending the fire — a man stoking the coals, a woman lifting the lid to check the stew. A small boy, maybe six or seven, sits on a worn blanket nearby, watching them. He is Johannes, when he was a child.

The mother stirs the pot. She says something to the boy — gentle, instructive, the way mothers correct their children. The boy laughs. The father smiles, watches his wife, watches his son. The fire crackles. The pot bubbles.

The image holds. *Warmth. Family. The pot doing the rest.*

The kitchen returns. Pip is sitting in the courtyard with the bowl in his hand. Johannes is across the fire, eating his own bowl, eyes half-closed, spoon paused midway. *He is also remembering, but in his own way — without seeing what Pip just saw. He is remembering the way the living remember.*

Pip swallows. **His strength meter rises** — a real, visible jump. *(Per the locked rule: strength starts at zero and grows as Pip collects memories. This memory is one of the larger lifts.)*

Johannes looks up. He doesn't see Pip — but he sees the bowl rising, and pauses, and smiles a little.

> JOHANNES (to no one): *Eat well, friend.*

Pätu eats from her own small dish. Tiny clinks of cat-spoon-on-pottery.

## Beat 12 — Henrik, the crew, the stew

Pip and Pätu return to the Mnemosyne with a covered pot of potjiekos. Johannes ladled extra. *"For your crew, ghost. You have a crew? Good. Always feed the crew."*

The kitchen is warm. Henrik is working — chopping, dicing, the steady rhythm of a chef's hands. He looks up when Pip drifts in.

> HENRIK: *Pip. You're back.*

Pip sets the pot on the counter. He explains briefly — Johannes in Saldanha Bay, the fire, the slow-cook, the brussels sprouts. He doesn't yet explain Iris. He doesn't yet have the words for that.

Henrik tastes the stew. He nods.

> HENRIK: *That's good. That's a real one. Tell me about the cook.*

Pip tells him. Johannes talked to himself. He talked to the cat. He sang. He forgot half the ingredients, found them again, kept stirring. Henrik smiles — rare, small.

> HENRIK: *The best cooks are the ones who can forget and still feed people.*

A pause. Then:

> PIP: *Henrik — there was a girl on the ship today. From a wreck. I helped her finish her meal, and she went home.*

Henrik stops. He sets down his knife.

> HENRIK: *...did she now.*

He looks at Pip for a long moment. He doesn't ask any of the questions Pip can see in his face. He picks up his knife again and goes back to chopping. But when he speaks, his voice is quieter than before.

> HENRIK: *Good. That's good, Pip. That was kind of you.*

He tosses Pätu a piece of fish from the cutting board. She catches it cleanly. He doesn't say anything else. The chapter ends with Pip in the kitchen, the pot of potjie cooling on the counter, Henrik's hands working on into the night, Pätu curled by the oven.

*(Quiet seed: Henrik does not say what he is thinking. The player who has been paying attention may guess. Henrik has been waiting a long time for someone he loved to finish their meal and go home. Hearing that another ghost-child got to do that — and that Pip was the one who helped — costs him something to hear and gives him something at the same time. The reveal of his quarters is still ahead.)*

## What this chapter teaches the player (mechanical literacy)

- **Memory-gifting works by selecting a memory from the journal and sending it.** The input is *focus + reach* — the player chooses *which* memory to give. (The exact UI is a Sprint 01-or-later design problem, but the literacy is established here.)
- **Memory-gifting can be defensive too.** The Mamlambo encounter teaches that warm memories sent into a hostile creature *cure* the encounter. Same mechanic that the climax will use at scale.
- **Object-memory works on people's loved objects.** Pip touches Iris's switchblade and sees her family. This is the first time object-memory has been used on something that *belongs to another ghost*. It will likely return.
- **Cross-chapter inventory keeps growing.** The candle, the matches, and now the switchblade. The player learns to trust that small objects pocketed in earlier chapters will be needed again.
- **The strength meter responds to meaning.** The potjiekos memory delivers a real strength jump — the player sees the meter respond to a *deeply* warm memory. Future chapters can calibrate against this.
- **Pätu can be the puzzle's solution.** She was the messenger to the chef (the brussels sprouts pendant). Her gameplay role keeps expanding — Ch3 (sniffing leads), Ch4 (active monster combat), Ch5 (envoy). She is not an accessory; she is a participant.
- **A chef who cannot see Pip is a chapter Pip cooperates with anyway.** New texture — Pip's helpfulness extends to the *invisible* assistance of the forgetful. The chef is fed by a ghost he never sees. Future chefs may be the same.

## What this chapter is NOT

- **Not a sad chapter, even though it has Iris.** Iris's resolution is *joyful* — she gets to go home to her family. The chapter's grief is held briefly (her translucent moment, the *I'd forgotten what his voice sounded like* line) and then released. The dominant register is *warmth recovered*.
- **Not the chapter where Pip realizes he is also stuck.** Iris is his foreshadow, not his mirror — yet. Pip will recognize his own version of her arc at the climax, but in Ch5 he is *helping*, not *seeing himself*. The bible-locked midpoint pivot — *moving on is reconnecting* — is planted as a lesson Pip carries forward. He does not yet know he will need it for himself.
- **Not where Iris recurs.** Iris moves on. She does not return as a recurring character (per bible canon). The switchblade is what she leaves behind. The ability is what she gave. That is enough.
- **Not the Henrik reveal chapter.** Henrik's recognition at the end (the quiet *did she now*) is a planted seed, not a reveal. The photograph chapter is still ahead (Ch6, Ch7, or Ch8 — TBD per `06-roadmap-and-open-questions.md`).
- **Not a folkloric-purist Mamlambo.** The Mamlambo in legend is a brain-sucker / hoarder / luck-stealer with various regional forms. This chapter uses the brain-sucker variant and gives the creature a *gentle horror* arc — recoiling from warmth rather than being defeated by force. *(Decision: folkloric figures are interpreted in service of the game's tone, not literally. To be logged in `06-roadmap-and-open-questions.md`.)*
- **Not where Pätu becomes a hero.** She has already had her hero moment (Ch4, routing the Karakoncolos). In Ch5 she is the *envoy* — the small bridge between Pip and the chef. Different register.
- **Not a chapter that explains memory-gifting in dialogue.** The ability is *shown*, not *named in conversation*. Pip doesn't say *"I just gave her a memory!"* He gives it; she goes home; the player understands. The journal records it; the player learns the literacy through use, not through tutorial.
