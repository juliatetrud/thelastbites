# Chapter 6 Outline — A Knot of Banana Leaves

The chapter where mercy costs something. Pip frees a bound soul and is, in gratitude, "infected" with painful memories that will haunt him for the rest of the game and catalyze his shadow at the climax. Indonesia is the port; the Pocong is the monster; a fast-talking young chef named Tirta is the warmth; the Henrietta thread continues, and the photograph of Erik appears for the first time.

This document is the **narrative outline** for the chapter. Implementation-grade detail (full dialogue, cinematic specs, journal-selection UI design) gets written separately when the chapter is being built.

**Working title:** *A Knot of Banana Leaves.* Recipe-themed convention from Ch3 onward.

---

## Position in the arc

Chapter 6 is the **first chapter of the post-midpoint half**. Per the bible (`01-story-bible.md`), Ch5 pivoted Pip from *tasting* to *understanding why food matters to the living*. Ch6 is where that pivot starts costing him.

- **The chapter rhythm holds, with stakes rising.** Wordless traversal → monster cleverness puzzle → chef encounter, plus a ship-side subplot beat at the end (Henrik). The structure is familiar; the emotional weight has escalated.
- **First deliberate use of memory-gifting *as a chosen tool* in a monster encounter.** Ch5 introduced memory-gifting (received from Iris, weaponized against the Mamlambo). Ch6 makes it the verb of the puzzle itself: Pip selects journal memories from a menu to soothe the Pocong before he can cut each knot. The same verb the climax will run on, taught one chapter at a time.
- **The infection mechanic is the chapter's biggest addition to the game's emotional vocabulary.** Pip leaves Ch6 carrying a third category of memories — *involuntary, transferred, not his own, not consensual* — that will surface as flashbacks across Ch7 and Ch8 and that the climax shadow will weaponize. This is the catalyst for the shadow per the locked Decisions Log update; it is *not* the shadow's source. The shadow is still Pip's accumulated grief; the Pocong's fragments activate it.
- **The "lean on warm memories to push back the dark" verb is taught.** First as outward action (soothing the Pocong) and then as inward action (Pip soothing himself when the flashbacks first hit, before and during Tirta's encounter). The same verb, two directions. The chapter's mechanical thesis.
- **The Henrik thread crosses a quiet threshold.** The Henrietta story (started Ch4, interrupted) continues here — and is interrupted again. The Erik photograph (planted Ch4 as object only) appears for the first time as an *image* — Henrik drops it; Pip picks it up; Pip does not yet recognize the boy in it. Recognition is reserved for the chapter where Pip meets Erik's ghost (TBD — Ch7 or Ch8).
- **Cross-chapter inventory pattern continues.** The switchblade Iris gave Pip in Ch5 is the chapter's load-bearing object. Used once, meaningfully. The candle and matches are still in Pip's pocket but unused in this chapter.

## Emotional arc

**What Pip carries in:** All five abilities through memory-gifting. The journal with five recipes (gravlaks/lefse, kodused kotletid, Sandy's red curry, Muhittin's muhammara, Johannes's potjiekos) and one earlier-Ch5 entry (Iris's family memory). Iris's father's switchblade in his pocket. The candle and matches still pocketed but unused. Pätu is with him. The lesson from Ch5: *moving on is reconnecting.*

**What Pip learns:** Mercy has weight. Helping a suffering soul costs the helper something — not as punishment, but as the *price of the thing*. Some grief is transferable, and once you've taken it on, you have to learn to live alongside it. The same warm memories you give to others are the ones you reach for to steady yourself. *Care for strangers is reconnection too* — Tirta's lemper memory completes the lesson Iris began. And quietly: Henrik had a son.

**What Pip carries out:** A new recipe (lemper). A taste-memory of Tirta's family wrapping snacks for hungry orphans. A stack of banana leaves the Pocong gave him (used in cooking with Tirta; some left over, pocketed for later). A handful of involuntary, dark memories from the Pocong — *the infection* — that will haunt him quietly across the rest of the game. The literacy to push them back with warm memories from the journal. And the first image of Erik, unrecognized.

The chapter's emotional center is the moment after the Pocong's gratitude — when Pip realizes what has just been transferred to him, and Pätu, sensing it, walks closer than usual to his side.

## Setting

Two settings, divided by the gangway, plus a brief return to the ship at the end:

**On the ship (lower decks).** A short wordless traversal through a humid stretch of the lower decks. Echo-tarsiers (pygmy tarsiers — small, shimmery, enormous-eyed) cling to the bulkheads and ceiling pipes. Their reflective eyes glow faintly. They blink sequentially in the dim. This is one of the ship's older incidents surfacing in the architecture; the great-tarsier story is held for later.

**In Kolonodale (Central Sulawesi).** A small port town. Wooden stilt-houses near the water. Coconut palms, the smell of charcoal and clove cigarettes, jackfruit and chili in the air. Banana groves at the edge of the town. The Pocong encounter happens at the edge of the banana grove — somewhere between the dock and the town proper, in a clearing with a cluster of old graves nearby. Tirta's kitchen is in the town itself: a small open-front warung with a thatched roof, a charcoal grill, an iron pan, and racks of pre-prepared ingredients. Late afternoon golden light, fading into evening across the chapter.

**Specific port:** Kolonodale, on Tomori Bay in Central Sulawesi. Real fishing town, smaller than Manado or Makassar, with the right sense of *off the main route*. Settled.

**Back on the ship (closing beat).** A short scene in the kitchen with Henrik. Evening. Salmon (per Ch4's quiet thread).

## The wordless traversal

Pip, Pätu beside him, moves through the lower deck of the Mnemosyne toward the gangway. The deck is humid in this latitude — condensation on the brass, sweat on the wood. The lights flicker low.

**Echo-creatures: pygmy tarsiers.** They cluster on pipes and ceiling beams in small groups of three or four, blinking those huge reflective eyes. They do not attack — they *stare*. When Pip passes near one, it tilts its head and follows him with its gaze. Bumping one drains a small amount of strength (the tarsier doesn't move; Pip does). Floating past avoids contact. The traversal isn't dangerous — it is *watched*.

**Hazards:** Tarsier-bumps drain strength sharply. No other hazards.

**The emotional bed:** *Pip is being seen. By many small things at once. He doesn't know yet that he is being prepared to see what he doesn't want to see.* The traversal foreshadows the chapter's central event without naming it.

**Length:** Brief. 60–90 seconds.

**No dialogue. No UI clutter. Just movement, atmosphere, the low hum of the ship and the soft sequential blinking of small wet eyes in the dark.**

## Beat 1 — Arrival at Kolonodale

Pip and Pätu cross the gangway into late-afternoon golden light. The port is small and busy — fishing boats unloading, cooking smoke from market stalls, children kicking a rolled-up sock down a dirt lane. No one notices the ghost-boy and the cat.

Pip walks. Pätu pads alongside, ears forward, tail relaxed. The town narrows toward the edge where the buildings give way to a banana grove. A path leads through it.

In the grove: the air cools. The light dims under the broad leaves. Old graves visible through the trees — small markers, weathered, kept clean by someone. Pätu's ears go back. She presses closer to Pip's side.

The path opens into a small clearing. In the clearing: a figure.

**No dialogue here. The atmosphere does the work. The chapter has shifted from town-warmth to grave-quiet in less than a minute.**

---

## Beat 2 — The Pocong

The figure is upright but bound — a tall shrouded shape, white burial cloth wrapped around the body, the head visible above with closed eyes that flicker open as Pip approaches. The shroud is tied at the feet with multiple knots. The cloth at the feet is bunched and tight. The Pocong cannot walk. He hops in small frantic jumps when he moves at all.

His hops are not aggression. They are *panic*. He has been trying to leave this clearing for a long time and cannot. When he sees Pip, he goes very still — a small ghost-child has come, and ghost-children sometimes notice what living people cannot.

The Pocong's voice is *spooky but not malicious*. Grieving. Old. Like wind through a partially open door. He speaks in slow, sung lines — not the way most people speak, but the way an old soul who has not had anyone to talk to in a long time has fallen back into.

> POCONG: *(softly)* *Little one. Little one. Will you cut?*

Pip looks down at the knots. Then at Pätu, who is sitting very still, ears alert but not flat. *Pätu does not hiss. The Pocong is not bad — he is lost.*

Pip reaches into his pocket and finds the switchblade. *(Iris gave it to him in Ch5. He has not used it yet. The blade flicks open.)*

The Pocong sees the blade. He inhales — relief, and dread that the relief will be taken away again. He cannot trust easily; he has been alone too long.

> POCONG: *Wait. Wait. I cannot — I cannot let you near. I am sorry. The cloth pulls. I jerk. The blade will slip. Soothe me first. Sing me something I can hold.*

He tilts his head, and his closed-eyes open just enough to show the player they are wet.

> POCONG: *Three knots. Three songs. Each true. Then we rest.*

## The puzzle — three knots, three riddles

A real cleverness puzzle, keyed to the Pocong's idiom. *He has been bound so long that he has forgotten how to be soothed by anything but the right memory in the right order. You sing him three things he can hold. Each one calms him enough for one cut.*

**Visual layout:** Side-scrolling clearing. The Pocong stands center; Pip enters from the left. A small banana-grove backdrop with the old graves at the edges of the frame. Late-golden light fading toward dusk over the course of the puzzle.

**On-screen instructional text** (appears once, when the puzzle begins):

> *Soothe the Pocong with a memory. Choose right and move on; choose wrong, and hear the Pocong's sad song.*

**The mechanic — for each knot:**

1. The Pocong speaks a riddle.
2. The journal opens. Pip's collected memories appear as small cards (one per chapter through Ch5). Player navigates with arrows; selects with space.
3. **Right memory:** The Pocong stills. His shoulders soften. His head tips back. The narration shifts — *He is ready.* Pip walks forward (arrows) and presses space at the Pocong's feet to cut the knot. The blade slices clean. One cord falls away. The Pocong exhales something that sounds like *thank you* in a language Pip doesn't know.
4. **Wrong memory:** The Pocong recoils — a small *agitation* animation, the cloth pulling tighter. A short, sad sung note plays — *the Pocong's sad song* — over the animation. The riddle reprints with a small additional hint folded into the next line. Pip picks again. **No life lost. No progress lost.** Per the no-fail rule.

**Three knots, three riddles, three keyed memories.** The order matters: each riddle is tuned to the Pocong's deepening loneliness, and the memories deepen in kind.

### Knot 1 — *the child being taught*

> *"Once a patient hand guided mine.*
> *A lesson given slow, in careful time.*
> *No one stays to help me learn —*
> *Show me a child being taught in turn."*

**Keyed memory:** Gravlaks (Ch1, 6a). Henrik as a small boy with his grandfather, learning to slice the salmon. *A patient hand on his. A lesson given slowly.* The child being taught.

**Wrong-answer hints (sample):**
- If lefse (6b) selected: *"Closer. But that is the one teaching, not the one taught. I want to be the small one again."*
- If muhammara (Ch4 orchard) selected: *"Closer. There were two children in that one. I want only one — and the hand that held his."*
- Other wrong selections: a more general *"That memory is warm. But it is not what I am asking for. Sing me a child who was taught."*

### Knot 2 — *the welcome of a stranger*

> *"I came to a house that was not my own.*
> *No name on my tongue, no kin, no home.*
> *The bowl was filled before I could speak —*
> *Show me a door opened to one who was weak."*

**Keyed memory:** Kodused kotletid (Ch2). Specifically the lunchbox taste-memory — young Leida sharing her mother's *kotletid* with the starving boy on the cold step. *No name in that house, no kin, no home; the bowl was filled.*

*(Cross-reference: this riddle requires the Ch2 outline's lunchbox taste-memory beat. Both docs updated together. See doc 06 Decisions Log.)*

**Wrong-answer hints (sample):**
- If Sandy's red curry (Ch3) selected: *"Closer. But that table was for kin. I had no kin there."*
- If Johannes's potjiekos (Ch5) selected: *"Closer. But those two were already a family. I wasn't anyone's, then."*
- Other wrong selections: *"Sing me one who was let in. Not one who already belonged."*

### Knot 3 — *the parents at the fire*

> *"Two stood close where the dark pressed in.*
> *No walls between them, only the night.*
> *They tended a flame that the cold could not reach —*
> *Show me the fire they kept alight."*

*(Variant: the player's earlier draft used the form "A family walked in a land they knew not / As darkness pressed close, the warmth they sought / Two worked as one against shadow and cold / Show me the flame they lit for all to behold." Either is acceptable; the tighter version above is more precisely keyed to Johannes's parents-at-the-fire image. **Open question: pick one when the chapter is built.**)*

**Keyed memory:** Johannes's potjiekos (Ch5). Johannes's parents tending a fire together — *two stood close where the dark pressed in.* The fire that the cold could not reach.

**Wrong-answer hints (sample):**
- If muhammara (Ch4 orchard) selected: *"Closer. But that one had a grandmother and two boys. I want only two, and the night around them."*
- If Iris's family memory selected: *"Closer. But that was a table indoors. I want the fire when there are no walls."*

## Beat 3 — The infection

After the third cut, the cloth at the Pocong's feet falls away entirely. He stands free for the first time in — long. His eyes open fully. He looks down at his own unbound feet for a long moment. He looks at Pip.

> POCONG: *(quietly, almost a child)* *Oh. Oh, thank you. Take these. I have nothing else.*

He reaches inside the loose folds of his shroud and pulls out a stack of large fresh banana leaves — somehow kept perfect through everything — and presses them into Pip's arms. Pip accepts them, surprised.

The Pocong hesitates. Then, as a final gesture of gratitude, he places his hand on Pip's small shoulder.

**A long single beat. Pip's face changes — not in pain, but in a way the player has not seen before. The screen darkens at the edges. Brief flashing fragments overlay the scene: a figure looking out from inside cloth, hands tied, watching a family on the other side of a window who cannot hear him calling; running shapes in the dark, fleeing him; a long stretch of being alone in a clearing at night while the world moved on without him.**

**The infection lands.**

Pätu's ears go *back*. She rises out of her sit, hisses — *not at the Pocong, but at the moment.* She presses her body against Pip's leg, urgent: *enough. let go. move on.*

The Pocong, suddenly aware, lifts his hand and steps back.

> POCONG: *I'm sorry. I'm sorry. I didn't know it would do that. I'm sorry.*

He bows his head. He turns. He walks — *walks, not hops* — slowly down the path away from the clearing, toward the graves at its edge, and then between the trees, and then into the dimming light, and is gone.

The clearing is quiet. Pip is standing with an armful of banana leaves and a journal that has just gained a small dark section in its back pages. The infection-memories are visible there — the player can see the journal has changed shape — but they are tinted differently from his own memories and from the memories he has chosen to receive. *(Cross-reference: journal UI design open; see doc 06.)*

Pip sways briefly. Pätu meows urgently.

Pip looks down at her. He breathes. He thinks of — *(the player is invited to think of)* — the warm memory at the top of his journal. *(Mechanically: a brief prompt or visual cue indicating Pip can summon a warm memory. The player selects one and the dark edges of the screen recede. The flashbacks quiet, for now.)*

This is the chapter's **mechanical-literacy beat**: the same memory-selection verb that soothed the Pocong now soothes Pip himself. *Outward and inward. The same warmth, two directions.*

## Beat 4 — The walk to Tirta

Pip walks back through the banana grove and into the town with the leaves in his arms and Pätu close at his side. Pätu is watching him carefully. She is taking the lead now — meowing at intervals, looking up at him, making sure he is still here.

The flashbacks return briefly along the way — short flickers, less intense than the first wave. *A figure inside cloth. A family on the wrong side of a window.* The player can summon another warm memory to push them back, or simply walk through them (the flashbacks pass on their own after a few seconds; using a memory makes them go faster and lifts the strength meter slightly). The player learns: *the warm memories are tools, not obligations.*

Pätu leads Pip toward a sound — a young woman's voice in the next street over, raised in cheerful crisis. *Aduh aduh aduh!* Pätu meows pointedly at Pip. *This way.*

## Beat 5 — Meeting Tirta

Tirta is a young Indonesian woman — late twenties, ponytail, apron streaked with chili oil, a dish towel over one shoulder. She is in the open front of her warung in mild theatrical distress. A pile of cooked sticky rice sits on her counter. A tray of cooked, shredded chicken sits beside it. A stack of dried-out, brittle, useless banana leaves sits next to *that*. She is poking the dried leaves with a finger as if hoping they will become flexible by force of will.

> TIRTA: *(to no one, to the universe)* *Why. Why today. There were a hundred today. A hundred. They were perfect this morning. I leave for one hour to get the chicken and they turn into — what is this — origami? Paper? Aduh.*

She picks up a leaf, tries to bend it; it cracks. She drops it back on the pile.

> TIRTA: *I will have to drive to my cousin's. My cousin who lives on the other side of the bay. My cousin who I owe money to. My cousin who will say "ohhh, Tirta, your leaves dried up, what a tragedy, by the way that two hundred thousand rupiah—"*

She breaks off because Pätu has just walked into her warung and sat down in the middle of the floor with the dignity of someone arriving for an appointment.

Tirta blinks at the cat.

> TIRTA: *...halo, kucing.*

She looks up. Pip is standing in the doorway with an armful of fresh, unblemished, perfect banana leaves.

Tirta's face goes through several expressions in approximately one second.

> TIRTA: *Are those — wait — are you — are those leaves real? You're a — okay. Okay! I have so many questions, but listen — listen — I will ask all of them in a minute. First. Will you trade me those leaves for the best lemper you have ever eaten in your afterlife? Yes? Yes. Yes. Come in. Come in, come in, come in.*

She is already taking the leaves from him, already moving back to the counter. Pätu jumps up onto a stool and watches with great satisfaction.

**Tirta is the chapter's tonal medicine.** Fast-talking, generous, deeply funny, completely unfazed by a ghost-boy. The kind of person who responds to the surreal by *getting on with it*. She does not interrogate Pip about being dead. She does ask his name. She does ask if he can taste lemper from the other side of being-alive. She is delighted to find out the answer is yes.

*(Cross-reference: Tirta is a real-person reference per the project's pattern of real chefs. Surname / specific Tirta TBD — see open questions.)*

## Beat 6 — Cooking the lemper

The chef puzzle. Format C from `02-game-design.md` (ingredient logic), Tirta's voice the vehicle.

The recipe — **lemper ayam** — built across the scene:

1. **Sticky rice (already cooked, cooling).** *"This is the body. Glutinous rice. You cook it with coconut milk and salt and pandan if you have it, which I do — see, pandan, here. Bayi. The baby. Smell that."*
2. **Shredded chicken filling (already cooked, in a bowl).** *"This is the heart. Shallot, garlic, lemongrass, coriander, kemiri — candlenut — galangal, salt, sugar, tamarind, coconut milk. You cook it down until it pulls apart with two forks. Like this. See? Like a love story — slow, slow, then suddenly tender."*
3. **Wrapping in the leaves.** *"And this is the dress. You take a leaf, you pass it over the flame — quick, quick, just enough to soften, you don't want to cook it — you lay rice flat, you put the chicken in the middle, you fold like this, like a little gift, and you tie."*

She demonstrates one. She hands Pip a leaf and gestures for him to try.

The cooking-puzzle is gentle:
- Pass the leaf over the flame the right number of seconds (not too long).
- Spread the rice the right thickness.
- Fold in the right order.
- Tie the right way.

Wrong choices are corrected with cheerful Tirta texture: *"Ah, no, no — like this — no like *this* — there, there, you've got it, see? You're a natural. You are a ghost natural. I have never said that sentence before."*

Pip ends up with a small wrapped lemper of his own. Tirta is making dozens behind him on the counter. She is humming.

The cooking continues for a while. They talk. *(Open: Tirta's cooking-scene voice, longer texture — to be developed.)*

**The recipe earned: lemper ayam.** Sticky rice with chicken filling, wrapped and tied in banana leaves. Unlocked on the recipe website at the chapter's end.

## Beat 7 — The taste-memory

Tirta steams a tray of finished lemper. She unwraps one. She passes it to Pip.

> TIRTA: *Eat. Tell me what you see.*

Pip eats. **Taste-memory triggers.**

The warung falls away. Pip is in a courtyard at night, not Tirta's, lit by hanging lamps. A long folding table covered in banana leaves and bowls and trays. A family of seven or eight at the table — Tirta among them, much younger, maybe sixteen. An older woman who must be her grandmother. Aunts. Uncles. Two small cousins. Everyone is wrapping lemper, hundreds of them, an assembly line of love. They are laughing. Tirta's grandmother is calling out instructions. Music plays from a phone propped against a thermos. The smell of pandan and coconut and clove cigarettes.

Pip notices: *they are not making these for themselves.* The wrapped lemper are being stacked into large, lined boxes. Tirta's mother is writing labels — *Panti Asuhan Cahaya Kasih*. Cahaya Kasih Orphanage. Tomorrow the lemper will go to the children there. Hundreds of children.

A small girl who is one of the cousins asks why they always make so many.

Tirta's grandmother — who is calm and warm and does not stop wrapping — answers:

> GRANDMOTHER: *Because someone else's child is also a child. And tomorrow, when they eat, they will know that someone they have never met thought about whether they were hungry. That is a kind of family. A small one. But a real one.*

The little cousin nods seriously and goes back to wrapping. The image holds for one more breath — the long table, the many hands, the lamps, the laughter — and fades.

Pip is back at Tirta's counter, holding a half-eaten lemper. Tirta is watching him.

> TIRTA: *(quieter than before, but smiling)* *You saw it?*

Pip nods.

> TIRTA: *My grandmother. I think about her when I cook these. She did this every — what was it — every two months. For years. She is gone now. I do it once a month. Smaller than her. But —*

She shrugs. She picks up another leaf.

> TIRTA: *Someone's child is hungry today.*

This is the chapter's **meal moment** for the climax pair. The lesson Iris began — *moving on is reconnecting* — extends here: *care for strangers is reconnection too.* A family Pip has never met has just become, briefly, his family. He carries them.

The journal entry is written: recipe component (lemper ayam → strength), memory component (Tirta's grandmother's courtyard → empathy).

## Beat 8 — The leftover leaves

Tirta gives Pip a small bundle of the unused banana leaves to take with him. *"In case you meet another chef. Pass it on."* She does not mean it cosmically. She means it practically. She is already wrapping more.

Pätu stretches and hops down from the stool. They say goodbye at the doorway.

> TIRTA: *Come back if you find your way back, ya? I have other recipes. Many. Many. And I have so many more questions for you, but next time. Next time.*

She waves them off. Pip steps out into the evening street with banana leaves under one arm and the lemper recipe in his journal.

He walks back toward the dock.

A flashback flickers in — *the figure in the cloth, the family on the wrong side of the window* — softer this time. Pip pauses. He summons, deliberately, the lamp-lit courtyard from the lemper memory: *the long table, the many hands.* The flashback fades.

He keeps walking. Pätu beside him.

## Beat 9 — Back on the ship: the photograph

Pip returns to the Mnemosyne and finds Henrik in the kitchen. Henrik is alone at the prep counter, between dinner services, doing nothing for the moment — just standing, hands flat on the wood, looking at something he is holding in one hand. As Pip enters, Henrik startles slightly. The thing in his hand falls — a small photograph — and slides face-down across the counter toward Pip.

Pip floats it back up to him. He looks at it on the way.

**The image:** A boy of roughly twelve. Dark hair, a slight gap between his front teeth, an open expression — the face of someone caught laughing. Standing in a kitchen Pip does not recognize, holding a wooden spoon almost too big for him. *(Erik. The player may suspect; Pip does not yet know.)*

Pip hands the photograph to Henrik. He does not ask. *(He has learned, from Ch4, not to ask.)*

Henrik takes it. He looks at it a moment longer. He puts it in a drawer.

Henrik does not speak for a beat. Then he says, with the careful evenness of someone who has decided to keep talking even though he could stop:

> HENRIK: *Henrietta liked banana. Strange thing. Norwegian woman, banana lover. She used to put it in things she shouldn't. Pancakes. Yes, fine. But also fish stews. Once or twice. We had a —*

He stops. The kitchen radio crackles to life — the second cook calling for him, dinner prep starting up. Henrik exhales.

> HENRIK: *Another day. Tell me about your port.*

Pip tells him about the lemper. He gives Henrik the bundle of leftover banana leaves.

> HENRIK: *Ah. Smart. These keep, you know. We will use them.*

He tucks them away. He nods at Pip — *good work* — and turns to the radio.

This is the **Henrietta-thread continuation beat**. The story continues; it is interrupted again. The pattern is now a structural device, not a single occurrence. *(Cross-reference: Ch4 outline, Ch7+ outlines TBD. See doc 06 Decisions Log.)*

The photograph is the **Erik-photograph beat**. Pip has now *seen* Erik's face. He does not know it. The recognition is reserved for the chapter where Pip meets Erik's ghost. *(Open question: which chapter that is. See doc 06.)*

## Beat 10 — The chapter's quiet end

Pip walks down the kitchen passage with Pätu. He stops at a porthole. Outside: the harbor lights of Kolonodale, fading; the open sea ahead.

A flashback flickers in — softer than before. *The clearing at night. The years of being alone.* Pip closes his eyes. He summons, deliberately, the lemper courtyard. The flashback fades, but not completely. It is *quieter*. He carries it now.

The chapter ends on Pip at the porthole, Pätu sitting beside him, the banana leaves stacked neatly somewhere off-screen, the journal in his pocket newly and permanently changed.

The porthole transition rolls. Outside the glass: a sea Pip will cross next. Indonesia behind him. Somewhere ahead, the next port.

## Memory pair for the climax

- **Monster moment (frightening):** The infection. The Pocong's hand on Pip's shoulder, the sudden inrush of someone else's grief — *being inside cloth, watching family through a window, calling and not being heard.* This memory sits in the dark section of Pip's journal. The shadow at the climax will play it back at full intensity. Pip will need to override it.
- **Meal moment (warm):** The lamp-lit courtyard. The long table, the many hands, the lemper for children Pip has never met. *Someone else's child is also a child.* This is the warm memory Pip has used twice in this chapter already (to soothe his own flashbacks); by the climax it will be one of his strongest tools.

The pair is unusual — for the first time, the *frightening* memory is one Pip didn't witness and didn't choose to take in. It is *given* to him, against his consent, by someone he was kind to. That asymmetry is the chapter's point.

## What this chapter teaches the player (mechanical literacy)

- **Memory-gifting is the verb of the back half of the game.** The player learned it from Iris in Ch5. They use it as a soothing tool in Ch6's monster puzzle — *outward*. They use it on Pip himself when flashbacks hit — *inward*. They will use it at the climax to override the shadow. The same verb, three escalating contexts.
- **The journal is now a tool, not just a record.** For the first time, the player is asked to *select* from journal memories under pressure. This makes every recipe Pip has earned matter individually. Future chapters will calibrate against this.
- **There is a cost to mercy.** Pip frees the Pocong by choice. The Pocong did not mean to harm him. The infection happens anyway, because gratitude has weight. The bible's no-fail rule still holds — *Pip cannot lose the puzzle* — but the chapter's narrative cost is real and persistent.
- **A third memory-category exists.** Until now Pip's journal held his own memories and gifted-or-received-with-consent ones. Now it holds *involuntary, transferred* memories. They are visible, distinct, and persistent. The journal UI must show them as their own section.
- **Warm memories push back painful ones.** Demonstrated mechanically across Beats 3, 4, 8, and 10. The same verb the climax will run on, taught at smaller scale.
- **A chef's "real-time" memory can be the warm memory.** Tirta's lemper memory is from her own young adulthood, not from her childhood as a watcher. The first chef whose memory is *recently lived adult life* rather than a child-watching-an-elder. The verb's emotional range widens again.
- **The Henrik subplot keeps growing without resolving.** Henrietta is more of a person now (banana, fish stews). Erik is now *visually* a person. The pattern of *interrupted Henrik story* is now a structural device the player can recognize.

## What this chapter is NOT

- **Not the chapter where the shadow is named or shown.** The chapter plants the catalyst — *involuntary painful memories now in the journal* — but does not introduce the shadow figure or the shadow's voice. That is reserved for the climax.
- **Not the Henrik reveal chapter.** Ch6 plants the *image* of Erik (the photograph in Henrik's hand) and continues the Henrietta thread. Recognition of Erik and the Henrik-quarters reveal are still reserved for a later chapter.
- **Not Erik's birthday.** The Erik-birthday ship-side subplot is provisionally Ch7. Ch6 does not preempt it.
- **Not a tragedy.** The chapter has weight, but it ends with Pip on the ship, with Pätu, with a new recipe and a new memory of strangers fed. The infection is a thread Pip will carry; it is not the chapter's whole shape.
- **Not a place where the Pocong is villainous.** The Pocong is a grieving soul. He thanks Pip. He apologizes. He walks away. The chapter's antagonist is *the cost of mercy*, not a person.
- **Not where Pip learns memory-gifting.** That was Ch5. Ch6 is where the player learns to *use* it as a chosen verb in a puzzle context.
