# Chapter 7 Outline — A Stew for the Smoke-Child

The second-to-last port. Brazil. The chapter where another spirit looks at Pip and sees the darkness inside him — first hostilely (the Boitatá), then compassionately (the chef-household of Joana and Beatriz). Pip is diagnosed; Pip is fed; Pip carries the lesson back to the ship and, in the kitchen the same night, uses it for the first time. He meets Erik. He becomes the conduit between Henrik and the son Henrik has been mourning for years.

This document is the **narrative outline** for the chapter. Implementation-grade detail (full dialogue, cinematic specs, puzzle mechanics) gets written separately when the chapter is being built.

**Working title:** *A Stew for the Smoke-Child.* Recipe-themed convention from Ch3 onward.

---

## Position in the arc

Chapter 7 is the **last port chapter** before the climax. The Ch5–Ch6 escalation continues: Ch5 introduced memory-gifting; Ch6 made it the verb of the puzzle; Ch7 makes it the verb of the *resolution to a hostile-but-not-evil guardian*, and then — in the chapter's closing beat — the verb of *Pip's first deliberate gift to someone he loves*. The climax's mechanic is rehearsed at scale, one chapter early.

- **The chapter rhythm holds, with a second wordless traversal added.** Wordless traversal on the ship → monster encounter at the dock → second wordless traversal into the forest → chef encounter in the village → return to the ship → ship-side subplot (Erik). Two traversals in one chapter is new but earned — the forest needs its own breath between the monster and the chef.
- **Cross-chapter inventory becomes load-bearing in a way no chapter has done yet.** The matches from Ch3 are the trigger that summons the Boitatá. They are also the timer of the encounter. Inventory creates the problem and inventory times the problem.
- **The shadow's foreshadowing crystallizes here.** The Boitatá is the first creature in the game to *look at Pip and see the darkness he carries.* The chef-household sees it again, more deeply, and gives Pip a diagnosis that is essentially the prophecy of Ch8.
- **Memory-gifting graduates from puzzle-verb to relationship-verb.** Ch6 used memory-gifting to soothe a monster. Ch7 closes with Pip using it to gift Erik's last birthday memory *to Henrik* — not as combat, not as puzzle-solving, but as love practiced. The verb crosses two ghosts and a living father. *Erik gives → Pip relays → Henrik receives.* The climax in Ch8 will run this same circuit at scale.
- **Pätu gets a peer.** A capuchin meets Pip and Pätu in the forest. First time another small, observant animal has chosen to help them. A gentle gift to Pätu before Ch8.
- **Pip's emotional ground shifts.** Ch7 is the chapter where the grief Pip has been outrunning is *named by another character for the first time.* Joana and Beatriz tell him plainly: the darkness predates his death. He doesn't cry. His outline blurs. He stays steady because Joana is steady. He leaves *steadier than he arrived* — not transformed, not resolved, but with new equipment for what's coming.

---

## Emotional arc

**What Pip carries in:** Six abilities through memory-gifting. The journal with six recipes (gravlaks/lefse, kodused kotletid, Sandy's red curry, Muhittin's muhammara, Johannes's potjiekos, Tirta's lemper) plus chef memories and the Iris-family memory. Candle (Ch1), matches (Ch3), switchblade (Ch5), banana leaves (Ch6) in his pocket. **The infection** — involuntary dark memories from the Pocong — quietly present, surfacing as flashbacks. Pätu. A twelve-year-old's worth of grief, mostly under the surface, getting closer to the surface.

**What Pip learns:** Two spirits can read his infection from the outside — first hostilely (the Boitatá), then compassionately (Joana and Beatriz). He is *visibly* carrying something now. The diagnosis introduces three truths he will not fully understand until Ch8: *there are two dark things in him, not one; the shadow has been growing for longer than he's been a ghost; the answer is giving, not escaping.* In the taste-memory, he sees the long chain of cooks-feeding-others stretching back through generations — and understands, without being told, that he is part of that chain. And, in the ship-side beat with Erik and Henrik, he learns the lesson by *doing it* — gifting a memory across a threshold someone couldn't otherwise cross.

**What Pip carries out:** A new recipe (Bahian moqueca). A taste-memory of generations of communities sharing this stew across time and place. The diagnosis. The closing image of Beatriz's hand in Joana's. The memory of Henrik's face the moment he received Erik's birthday gift through Pip. **A steadiness he didn't have when he arrived.** A sense that he has a job to do and the resource to do it with. Pätu walking ahead of him with her chest puffed out.

The chapter's emotional center is the moment in Henrik's kitchen when Pip gifts Erik's memory to Henrik — and Henrik, for one breath, sees himself through his son's eyes.

## Setting

Four settings, sequential:

**On the ship (departure, brief).** Pip leaves the Mnemosyne at dusk. The lower-deck traversal is short — humid, dark, mosquitoes.

**The dock at the Brazilian port (night).** A small wooden dock in **Alter do Chão, Tapajós**. Locked 2026-06-01. The dock is functional, not picturesque: stacked crates, coiled ropes, a few moored fishing boats, the smell of river water and tar. Beyond the dock, the forest begins immediately. No streetlights. Pip lights a match. The Boitatá unfurls.

**The forest path (night).** After the Boitatá lets Pip pass, the path leads into the trees. Dense canopy. Phosphorescent fungi at the base of trunks. Distant frog-and-insect chorus. A capuchin meets them at a fork in the path and chitters at them until they follow. Light hazards along the way — fallen logs to jump, the occasional mosquito cloud. Pip is nervous, Pätu is alert, the capuchin is unhurried.

**Joana and Beatriz's house (night).** A small wooden house raised on stilts at the edge of the forest. A clay-tile roof. Open windows. Warm light spills through the doorway. Inside: a single room serving as kitchen and living space, a low fire in the corner hearth, a clay pot already heating, ingredients spread on a wooden counter. Pätu finds a windowsill.

**Back on the ship (closing scene).** Henrik's kitchen on the Mnemosyne. Late evening. Henrik is alone with Erik's photograph, lost in memory of Erik's last birthday. Pip enters intending to share his day. Everything changes.

## The first wordless traversal — ship to dock

Pip leaves the Mnemosyne at dusk. Pätu beside him.

**Echo-creatures: mosquitoes.** Dense, slow-moving clouds that drift through the lower-deck corridors. They hum constantly. They don't bite Pip (he is a ghost), but they swarm into Pätu's face and she swats at them irritably. The mosquitoes are the chapter's signal that the air is *humid, heavy, alive* — the tropics have arrived.

**Hazards:** Walking through a mosquito cloud slows Pip; Pätu hisses and falls behind, then catches up.

**The emotional bed:** *Pip has done this six times now. He knows the rhythm. He is no longer afraid of the corridors; he is afraid of what comes after them.* The Pocong infection from Ch6 surfaces as a flicker — for two seconds, the corridor is replaced with the inside of a banana-leaf cloth, looking out at a family through a small gap, calling and not being heard. Pip blinks. The corridor returns. Pätu rubs against his ankle. He keeps walking.

**Length:** Brief. 45–60 seconds.

## The Boitatá at the dock

*Pip and Pätu step off the gangway onto the dock. The air smells of river water. The dock is dark — the moon is behind clouds. The boat lanterns are out.*

*Pip pulls out the matches from Ch3. He needs to see where he's going. Pätu meows softly — she can see fine, but she understands.*

*Pip strikes a match.*

*The world goes still.*

*The dock's water-surface ripples outward without wind. Something is rising under it. The match-flame in Pip's hand is the only point of light. Pätu hisses for the first time in three chapters and backs away to a piling.*

*The Boitatá unfurls from the river. He does not splash. He does not roar. He pours upward out of the water in a coil that is impossible — too long, too smooth, too bright. His body is fire shaped like a serpent. His eyes are lanterns the size of dinner plates. He has been hunting arsonists for centuries.*

*He looks down at Pip and the match in Pip's hand.*

*Pip cannot move.*

*The Boitatá exhales — not a roar, an exhale — and the breath catches the match-flame and throws it outward in three directions. Three small fires light around Pip: one on a coil of rope, one on a tarred wooden plank, one on a piece of sailcloth.*

*The match in Pip's hand is now half its size.*

*Around Pip, a cloudy bubble forms — smoke and amber heat-haze in a dome maybe ten feet across. Pip is inside it. The Boitatá's head is at the dome's edge.*

> BOITATÁ: *(low, ancient)* "Little ghost. You carry a fire I cannot see. It is not the kind I burn. Something dark rides with you. I do not know its name."

*The fires begin to creep toward Pip.*

*A wooden bucket sits at the edge of the dock. The dome contains river-water on one side, where the dock meets the water. The puzzle clicks into place: Pip has to fill the bucket and put out the fires before the match in his hand burns out. He doesn't yet know what happens if it does.*

### The puzzle

Bucket-and-water, simple-but-timed. The cleverness is the small *aha* — *oh, I need to fill the bucket first.*

- **Three fires**, each lit on a different surface (rope, wood, sailcloth). Water works on all of them. The variety is visual, not mechanical.
- **The match is the timer.** The match in Pip's hand burns down through the puzzle. When the match goes out, the spell ends — but if any fire is still lit, the *pain made real for ghosts* mechanic kicks in. Pip sneezes (his body rejecting the spell) and bursts out of the dome. The puzzle resets. Match restored. New attempt.
- **Pip is in the dome the whole time.** The dome is a few square feet of dock around him. He can reach the bucket, the water's edge at the dome boundary, and each fire.
- **The Boitatá speaks once per attempt, mid-puzzle.** Each attempt, his line is slightly different — sharper, more uncertain. He is reading the darkness in Pip and growing more troubled by it. By the second or third attempt, the player understands that *the Boitatá is not just testing Pip; he is studying him and not liking what he sees.*
- **The sneeze:** First attempt the sneeze is surprising — small comic-air in a heavy beat. Second attempt the player anticipates it. Third+ attempt the player is frustrated *for* Pip — the rhythm has become grim. The chapter's tone graph for this scene: *sneeze 1 funny → sneeze 2 less funny → sneeze 3+ frustrated.*
- **No failure state.** Pip cannot die. The retry loop is just *try again,* same as every other puzzle in the game.

### Resolution

*All three fires are out. The match is almost burned to Pip's fingers. The dome's edge is dimming.*

*The Boitatá lowers his head. He regards Pip for a long moment.*

> BOITATÁ: "Pass, little ghost. But you carry something I have not seen before. Be careful of it. Or it will eat you."

*He does not bless Pip. He does not move out of the way kindly. He simply lifts himself from the dock and slides back into the river without ripple. The dome dissipates. The night is dark again.*

*Pip blows out what's left of the match. He pockets it. His hand is shaking.*

*Pätu jumps down from the piling and walks ahead of him toward the forest path. She does not look at him. She is letting him collect himself without being watched.*

## The second wordless traversal — forest path

*The path begins where the dock ends. Pip floats slowly between the trees. Pätu walks ahead.*

*The forest is loud. Frog calls. Cicada whirr. Something bigger calling and being answered.*

*A capuchin drops from a branch and lands on the path in front of them. Small, sharp-eyed, fur tipped white at the ears. He chitters at Pip and then at Pätu.*

*Pätu sits and considers him. He considers her. They have a conversation Pip cannot follow.*

*The capuchin turns and walks down a fork in the path. He turns back, chitters again — clearly impatient. Pätu stands and follows. Pip floats after.*

**Hazards:** Light. A fallen log to jump over (the capuchin sails over it; Pätu hops it; Pip floats). A small mosquito cloud (Pip drifts through; Pätu growls). A patch of phosphorescent fungi the path threads between. The chapter's *quiet, watched walk.*

**The emotional bed:** *Pip is being led somewhere. He doesn't know where. He is tired and he is afraid and he is following a monkey through a forest at night. Pätu is unbothered, which is the only reason Pip is not panicking. Pätu's choices are Pip's barometer.*

*Two minutes into the walk, the infection flickers again. A second Pocong-fragment — different from the first. A hand reaching toward a child. The hand stops mid-gesture. Pip can feel the wanting in the hand, the not-completing. The forest returns. Pip stays floating. Pätu does not turn around. The capuchin does not slow.*

**At the end of the path:** Smoke. The smell of cooking. A small wooden house raised on stilts at the edge of the trees. Light through the open doorway. The capuchin stops, chitters one last time, and disappears into the canopy. Pätu pads up the path toward the house. Pip follows.

**Length:** Brief. 90–120 seconds.

## The chef encounter — Joana and Beatriz

*Pip and Pätu come up the last few feet of path. Two women are already standing in the doorway when Pip floats up. They have been waiting — or, more accurately, they have been **here**, and now Pip has arrived.*

*Beatriz steps forward first. She is in her sixties, gray-streaked hair pulled back, an apron over a faded cotton dress. Joana stands a half-step behind her — taller, browner from the sun, hands still wet from washing something. Beatriz looks at Pip and her face softens.*

> BEATRIZ: "Oh. Oh, sweetheart. Come in. Come into the light."

*Pip hovers at the threshold. Pätu pads in past him, tail up, like she's been here before.*

*Beatriz turns to a small table just inside the door. She strikes a match — and Pip flinches; the match-trigger is still raw — and lights a single tall candle. She sets it on the table between herself and the doorway where Pip floats.*

> BEATRIZ: "There. Now we can see each other properly."

*Joana watches from behind Beatriz's shoulder. Her eyes have not left Pip. She is reading him the way she reads the forest — slowly, completely. She nods, once, very small. Beatriz feels the nod without looking back.*

> BEATRIZ: "My boy. You carry a darkness within you."

*Pip's outline blurs at the edges. He doesn't say anything. Pätu, on the floor between Beatriz's feet, meows — a small, alert sound.*

> BEATRIZ: "There is dark where there is light, child. That is the way of things. But there is something borrowed in you too. Something that was not yours. Someone else's grief, sitting on top of yours."

*Joana steps forward now, beside Beatriz. She is looking at the space just above Pip's chest — where, if he had a heart, his heart would be.*

> JOANA: "Two things, not one. I want you to know the difference. It matters."

*Pip looks down at the floor. His outline blurs more.*

*Beatriz softens her voice further.*

> BEATRIZ: "Hey. Hey, look at me. We're not going to leave you with this. Come — let's cook you a meal. Moqueca will get you on your feet again..."

*She catches herself. A small wry smile.*

> BEATRIZ: "...or — rather — feeling right again. Yes? Come."

*She turns toward the kitchen. Joana extends her hand — open, palm up — near Pip's shoulder. Not touching. Just close. Pip looks at the hand. After a moment he floats forward, following them in.*

*Pätu jumps up to the windowsill above the kitchen counter and settles in to watch.*

---

### The cooking, the teaching

*The kitchen is one room with the rest of the house. A clay pot is already heating over a low fire in the corner hearth. Onions, garlic, peppers, tomatoes, a fish wrapped in newspaper, a bowl of cilantro, a green bottle of dendê oil, a can of coconut milk, half a lime. The smell is already starting.*

*Joana sets the fish on the cutting board. Beatriz hands Pip a bunch of cilantro and a small bowl.*

> BEATRIZ: "Pick the leaves off. Just the leaves. The little stems are fine, the big ones go in the compost."

*Pip floats up to the counter. His hands work the cilantro. He is doing something. His outline steadies a little.*

> JOANA: "You're worried about what we said. The two darks."

*Pip nods, eyes on the cilantro.*

> JOANA: "It's all right to be worried. I'd be worried too."

*A beat. She halves an onion and starts dicing.*

> JOANA: "The one that's borrowed — that was put into you by someone who was hurting. Someone who needed to share what they were carrying. That can be a good thing, even when it doesn't feel good. It means you were kind to them."

*Beatriz lights another burner. She glances at Pip's outline, which is steadying.*

> BEATRIZ: "The other one — that one's older. That one has been with you since before you were a ghost, sweetheart. You've been carrying it your whole short life. Most boys don't notice they're carrying it. You're noticing because the borrowed one woke it up."

*Pip's hands pause in the cilantro. Then keep going.*

> JOANA: "The bad news is — we can't take it out. It's part of you. We can't pull it out the way Beatriz used to pull splinters."

> BEATRIZ: *(dry)* "I was very good at splinters."

> JOANA: *(small smile)* "She was. Pass me the garlic."

*Beatriz passes garlic. Joana starts smashing cloves with the flat of her knife.*

> JOANA: "But here is the good news, Pip. The dark in you — it isn't your enemy. It's part of you. If you fight it, you fight yourself. If you run from it, it runs with you. Neither one works."

*She slides the smashed garlic into the pot. The kitchen smells extraordinary.*

> JOANA: "The way to answer the dark is not to escape it. It's to give. To give the warmth you've been collecting. Every meal you've eaten on this journey — every person who has fed you — they put something warm into you. You've been carrying it without knowing."

> BEATRIZ: "Pass the cilantro, sweetheart."

*Pip slides the bowl toward her. He's listening hard.*

> BEATRIZ: "When the dark comes for you — and it will — you don't push it away. You give it the warmth. You feed it the way you've been fed."

*A pause. Joana watches the pot. Beatriz watches Pip.*

> JOANA: "Light and dark hold each other, Pip. You can't kill one without the other. The thing to do is keep giving warmth, so that the dark inside you stays in balance with everything you've been given. That's the only way I know."

> BEATRIZ: "Try the broth."

*She lifts the wooden spoon from the pot, blows on it, and brings it close to Pip's mouth. Pip leans toward it.*

---

### The taste-memory cinematic

*When the broth meets Pip's mouth, the kitchen falls away.*

*He sees:*

*A coastal Bahian kitchen, generations ago. A woman with strong hands, an iron pot, fish from a morning's catch, dendê oil from a clay jar. She ladles the moqueca into small bowls. Children waiting at the doorway. She passes the bowls out one by one.*

*A different kitchen, decades later. A small apartment. A woman cooking moqueca for her neighbor — the neighbor has had a hard week. She brings the pot to the neighbor's door.*

*A wartime kitchen. One fish. The cook stretches it to feed twelve. No one goes hungry. The cook eats last.*

*A celebration. A pot the size of a child. A whole community around it. Someone laughs. Someone cries from joy. The food keeps coming out of the pot.*

*A modern kitchen. A young woman teaching her wife to make moqueca for the first time. Both of them laugh at how much dendê is too much.*

*A fishing village at dusk. The catch comes in. Three families share one pot. They eat outside under hanging lights.*

*The visions are not in chronological order. They overlap. They blend. The constant is the **gesture** — fish going into pot, broth going into bowls, bowls into hands. The chain is unbroken. The chain is older than anyone in it. The chain is what survives.*

*Pip sees — and understands without being told — that **this is what he is part of.** Every cook who has ever fed someone. The same gesture, repeating, across generations. He has been a link in it without knowing it. He still is.*

*The vision lingers on a final image: a small boy at a kitchen table, hands open, receiving a bowl. The boy could be Pip. The boy could be any of them.*

*The vision fades.*

---

### After the taste-memory

*Pip is back in the kitchen. The pot is bubbling. Joana is watching him with a steady, knowing face.*

> JOANA: "Did you see them?"

*Pip nods, very slowly.*

> JOANA: "Good. Now you know. You're not alone in this, even when you feel alone. You're part of something very long."

> BEATRIZ: "And now — eat. The taste does most of the work; the rest is just calories, which you don't need. But still. Stay with us a little. Let it sit in you."

*Pip stays. He hovers near the counter. He doesn't speak much, but his outline has steadied, then started — faintly — to glow warmer. Joana sees it and squeezes Beatriz's hand under the table without looking.*

---

### Departure

*Time later. Pip is at the door. Pätu is already on the path, looking back at him.*

> JOANA: "Go on, smoke-child. You have somewhere to be."

> BEATRIZ: "Remember. Give. Don't escape. Don't fight. **Give.**"

*Pip nods. He turns to go. At the edge of the porch he turns back once.*

*Joana and Beatriz are standing in their doorway, watching him. Beatriz's hand is in Joana's. Joana's other hand is at her own heart. They are very still and very settled, two women in a kitchen at the edge of a forest at evening.*

*Pip carries the image with him.*

*Pätu walks ahead of him on the path. Her chest is puffed out. She has decided that Pip is going to be all right, and she is going to make sure of it.*

## Return to the ship

A brief beat. Pip and Pätu walk back through the forest path (capuchin gone, mosquitoes thinner, the path easier). They reach the dock — the Boitatá is not there. The dock is just a dock again. They walk up the gangway. The Mnemosyne is quiet.

Pip floats toward Henrik's kitchen to share his news.

## The Erik beat — Henrik's kitchen, late evening

*The kitchen is warmly lit. Henrik stands at the counter with the photograph from Ch6 in his hands. He has been holding it for a while. He is alone — as far as he knows.*

*Henrik's face is held. His eyes are glassy with tears he is not letting fall. He is thinking of Erik's last birthday — Erik tearing the wrapping paper, screaming with glee at the gift inside. The good memory, deliberately chosen. The years close to Erik's death he sobbed; now he tries to hold the joy instead. It is a discipline.*

*Pip floats into the doorway.*

*Pip and Erik see each other at the same instant. Erik — eleven, alive-looking, standing behind Henrik with his hand resting on his father's back — looks up. Pip sees him. Pip is still processing what he is seeing.*

*Erik's face lights up. He waves with both hands, bouncing on the balls of his feet.*

> ERIK: "Finally someone who can see me!"

*Henrik does not react to Erik's voice — he cannot hear it. He is still facing the photograph.*

*Pip catches up to the word **Erik**, word by word, as his mind matches the boy in front of him to the boy in the photograph he saw in Ch6.*

> PIP: "Erik?"

*Henrik's head turns first toward where Pip's voice is aimed — over Henrik's own shoulder, toward the empty air behind him. Henrik sees nothing. His face changes. His eyes settle on Pip.*

*Henrik does not speak. His face asks the question.*

*Pip is in the doorway, looking past Henrik at Erik. He has to break the silence. He does.*

> PIP: "He's right here, Henrik."

*Henrik's first sound is an intake of breath. Not a word. A body acknowledging what it has just received.*

*Then —*

> HENRIK: "Who?"

*The word comes out in disbelief. He almost cannot say it.*

*Pip looks at Erik. Erik nods at him — go on.*

> PIP: "Your son. Erik. He's here. He's behind you. He says he visits every year on this day."

*Henrik's hand finds the counter and stays there.*

> HENRIK: "His birthday..."

*Erik bursts in then — he cannot hold it anymore.*

> ERIK: "Tell him I love him! Tell him I never left!"

*Pip stumbles a little. He has never done this before.*

> PIP: "He — he says he loves you. He's saying he never left. He never left, Henrik."

*The last sentence — without **he says** in front of it — lands as Pip's own testimony.*

*Henrik turns around slowly. He cannot see Erik. But he turns to face the air where Pip's eyes have been looking. He faces his son. His face is wet now.*

> HENRIK: "Erik. My boy. I — I have missed you every day."

*Tears spill over. He does not wipe them.*

> HENRIK: "I have wondered. Every year on this day, I have wondered. I am so sorry I could not see you. I am so sorry."

*Erik shakes his head furiously, eyes filling, smiling.*

> ERIK: "Tell him not to be sorry! Tell him I'm okay! Tell him I've been with him!"

*Pip relays each one. Henrik listens. He does not interrupt. He is holding the counter.*

*A long pause. Henrik looks at the air where Erik is — close, but not quite right. Off by a few inches.*

> HENRIK: "I wish I could see you. Even once more. Just to see your face."

*Erik looks at Pip. He doesn't know how to fix this. He looks devastated for his father.*

*And Pip — who has just spent the day at Joana and Beatriz's kitchen learning that the answer is to give, who carries six chefs and seven monsters and a chain of cooks across centuries inside him — Pip has an idea.*

> PIP: "Erik. Show me your favorite memory."

*Erik blinks. Then his face changes. He understands.*

*He closes his eyes. The memory rises in him — Pip can see it, the way Pip's gifted memories rose in Iris.*

*Pip receives it. Holds it. Turns to Henrik.*

> PIP: "Henrik. He's giving you something. I'm — I'm going to pass it to you. Hold still."

*Henrik nods, barely.*

*Pip reaches — not touching, because he is a ghost, but close. The memory leaves Pip's hand the way warmth leaves an open palm. It enters Henrik.*

*For one moment, Henrik is not in the kitchen.*

*He is at Erik's last birthday. The living room of the old house. The cake. The wrapped gift in his own hands, holding it out. But the perspective is wrong — he is **looking up** at himself. He is **inside Erik**, seeing the gift come toward him, feeling his own small body bouncing with excitement, feeling his throat about to scream with joy. He sees his own face — younger, beaming, alive in a way he has not been in many years — leaning down over him.*

*The father his son was looking up at.*

*The memory plays through. The wrapping paper. The scream of glee. His father's laugh. The wholeness of being loved by him.*

*The kitchen returns.*

*Henrik is sobbing now. Quietly, with his hand on the counter, not trying to hide it.*

*Erik is crying too — happy tears.*

*Pip is crying — no, Pip is not crying. His outline is blurring, soft and warm, and his eyes are full, but he does not cry. *(That is saved for Ch8.)* He stays steady because he has work still to do.*

*A long quiet.*

*Erik wipes his eyes with the back of his hand. Looks at Pip.*

> ERIK: "Thank you."

*Pip nods.*

> ERIK: "I have to go now. The veil doesn't let me stay long. But — I'll see him again. I'll see you again. I'll see all of us. I promise."

*Pip relays this last to Henrik.*

> HENRIK: "Tell him I love him. Tell him — tell him I'll be all right now."

*Pip relays it. Erik nods. He looks at his father one more time — really looks, the way someone looks who knows they have a year to wait — and then he is gone. Not faded. Not walked away. Gone, the way a flame goes when it is blown out.*

*The kitchen is quiet.*

*Henrik turns to Pip. He cannot speak for a moment. Then —*

> HENRIK: "Pip. Thank you."

*Pip nods. He is exhausted now. He has done something today he did not know he could do.*

*Henrik finally lets the photograph rest on the counter. He takes a long breath.*

> HENRIK: "Tell me about your day."

*Pip floats up onto the counter near him — not sitting, just close — and starts to tell him about the Boitatá, and about Joana and Beatriz, and about the moqueca. Henrik listens. Pätu jumps up to the counter beside Pip. The candle Beatriz lit at the doorway is, somewhere in Pip's memory, still burning.*

*The chapter ends here. Quietly. Two people in a kitchen at night.*

## What this chapter is NOT

- **Not a chapter where Pip becomes a medium.** Pip relays Erik. He does not channel. The verb is *passing along what he heard*, not *speaking with another's voice.* The mechanic stays in the register the game has been using.
- **Not a chapter that depicts any specific Indigenous culture or practice.** Joana and Beatriz are a modern Brazilian household. Joana's magic is the game's invention. Their politics are general Brazilian environmentalism (Chico Mendes-era through-line), not the specific Munduruku struggle. See `research/munduruku-tapajos-iracema-research.md` for the institutional memory of why this chapter pivoted away from that scope.
- **Not a chapter with a reflex puzzle.** The Boitatá's bucket-and-water beat is timed, but the difficulty is the small *aha* of figuring out the bucket. Once the player has the system, they can clear it. No fail state. Just retry.

## Memory pair for the climax (Ch7 contribution to Ch8)

- **Monster moment:** The Boitatá's spell. *Pain made real for ghosts. The sense of being seen and not understood. The dim of the match. The certainty that even other spirits can sense the wrongness Pip carries.* This is the memory the shadow will play first in Ch8's reverse-chronological override.

  2. *Three people cooking together at Joana and Beatriz's fire.* Pip's hands picking cilantro; Beatriz tending the flame; Joana smashing garlic; the lessons woven through.
  3. *Henrik's face the moment he received Erik's birthday gift through Pip.* The act of gifting a memory across a threshold someone couldn't otherwise cross. This is the rehearsal of the climax's verb at full emotional weight.

The Ch7 meal moment is unusually large because the chapter is unusually large. The shadow will play *one* of these against Ch7's monster moment; the others compound in Pip as warmth-reserves the player has watched him collect.

## Foreshadowing planted

- **The Boitatá saw what Joana and Beatriz also saw, but did not understand it.** First a hostile reading, then a compassionate one. Ch8's shadow is what is being read. The player understands this even if Pip doesn't fully.
- **The chain of cooks across generations.** The taste-memory establishes that the chain goes back. The bottom-of-the-well memory in Ch8 — Babcia teaching small Pip the fold — closes the loop. *Babcia learned from her grandmother who learned from hers.* Pip is one link in a chain that goes back further than him and continues past him.
- **The gift to Henrik is the rehearsal of the gift to the shadow.** Pip gifts Erik's memory across a threshold to a living person who could not otherwise have it. In Ch8, Pip will gift his warm memories across a threshold inside himself, to a part of himself that cannot otherwise have them. Same verb, scaled up.
- **Erik exists across the veil and can return briefly.** The Ch8 reunion is now staged: Pip's parents and Erik will come through the veil to escort Pip across. Erik has the precedent, established here.
- **Henrik is not the wise mentor any longer.** He is a grieving father who has been carrying his loss alone. Ch8 will need him to receive again — to find Pip, to be taught the pierogi fold, to release.

## Recipe for the companion site

**Bahian moqueca.** Coconut milk, dendê oil (palm oil), fish (firm white fish — grouper, halibut, mahi), peppers, tomatoes, onion, garlic, cilantro, lime. Slow-cooked in a clay or heavy pot. The recipe site sources to Bahian tradition with proper credit to regional cooking. Notes that there are regional variations (including the Pará-style *moqueca paraense* with tucupi and jambu) and that this version is the more accessible one for cooks outside Brazil.
